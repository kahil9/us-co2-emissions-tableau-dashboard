**U.S. Greenhouse Gas Emissions Analysis**

**Overview**
This project analyzes greenhouse gas emissions in the United States using interactive Tableau visualizations. The goal is to explore emission trends across sectors and time periods, identify major contributors to CO₂ output, and highlight data-driven strategies for reducing overall emissions.

The dashboard transforms raw environmental data into actionable insights that can support sustainability planning, policy decisions, and climate risk analysis.

**Objective**
- Analyze U.S. greenhouse gas emission trends across sectors
- Identify the primary contributors to CO₂ emissions
- Explore patterns and changes in emissions over time
- Present insights through an interactive Tableau dashboard
- Support data-driven strategies for emissions reduction

**Key Insights**
- Emissions are not evenly distributed across sectors, with certain industries contributing significantly more than others
- Energy related activities remain one of the largest drivers of CO₂ output
- Transportation and industrial sectors show consistent and meaningful contributions to total emissions
- Temporal trends suggest fluctuations in emissions that highlight opportunities for targeted reduction strategies
- Data indicates that focused interventions in high-emission sectors could significantly reduce overall greenhouse gas output
